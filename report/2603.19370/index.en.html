1. Quick overview of the paper

One-sentence summary: VAMPO treats multi-step denoising of diffusion-based Video Prediction Model (VPM) as an optimizable strategy, using verifiable rewards on expert latent dynamics and post-training VPM with GRPO, thereby improving the visual dynamics accuracy of the video action model and ultimately improving robot action generation.

Difficulty rating: ★★★★☆. Need to understand diffusion video model, EDM/Euler sampler, DDPO/GRPO, latent video-action model, CALVIN long-range evaluation and robot VLA training process.

Keywords: Video Action Model, Visual Dynamics, Diffusion Policy Optimization, GRPO, Latent Reward.

2. Motivation

2.1 What problem should be solved?

The core idea of the Video Action Model (VAM) is that the Video Prediction Model first predicts the future visual representation based on current observations and language instructions, and then the Action Generation Model converts the representation into an action sequence. This paradigm has an explicit "future visual dynamics" middle layer than directly mapping from current observation/language to action.

The problem is that VPM is often trained with diffusion likelihood surrogate to optimize the global plausible generation of the target biased data distribution. Robot manipulation is more sensitive to certain local details: slightly different object poses, gripper contact one frame earlier or one frame later, and small gaps in spatial relationships may cause AGM to act differently. The paper calls this the objective mismatch between generative training and action-centric deployment.

2.2 Limitations of existing methods

Pixel-level VPM can intuitively output future images or videos, but it requires a large amount of control over weakly relevant information such as texture and lighting synthesis, and the computational overhead is also high. Latent-level VPM is more efficient, can compress part of the nuisance variability, and obtain task-related dynamics from early denoising features; therefore, this article also adopts the latent-level paradigm.

However, latent-level does not automatically solve the training target problem. The paper clearly points out in the Preliminary that the VPM output may be coherent as a whole, but have minor errors in pose, contact timing, and fine-grained spatial relations; after these errors are consumed by AGM, they will be amplified near the decision boundary, leading to incorrect actions and compounding failures.

2.3 The solution ideas of this article

The idea of VAMPO is to directly optimize the predicted visual dynamics of VPM instead of just relying on likelihood surrogate. The specific method is to regard the denoising step as a policy action, use the consistency between the final predicted latent and the expert future latent as the terminal reward, and use policy-gradient-style post-training to change the sampling distribution of VPM.

4. Detailed explanation of method

4.1 Method overview

The paper uses latent-level video action model. The data sample contains the current observation $o$, the verbal instruction $l$, the expert action sequence $\mathbf{a}$, and the target future video clip $N$. VPM uses $c_v=(o, l)$ as the condition to predict the future latent $\hat{x}_0$ and intermediate visual feature $\hat{h}$; AGM then uses $c_a=(\hat{h}, l)$ as the condition to generate the action $\hat{a}_0$.

VAMPO's post-training is only for VPM: start with noisy latent and roll out multiple candidate denoising trajectories. In the first step, SDE is used to generate stochastic exploration, and in subsequent steps, deterministic ODE is used to maintain video trajectory consistency. Each candidate finally gets $x_{\sigma_0}^g$, and the reward is calculated with the expert latent $x_0=\mathcal{E}(N)$; GRPO updates the VPM with the normalized advantage within the group.

Figure 2. VAMPO training paradigm: The hybrid sampler of the first step SDE and subsequent ODE generates candidate future latent; L1 reward and cosine reward together constitute verifiable reward; GRPO pushes VPM to more precise visual dynamics.

4.2 Method evolution

Pixel-level VAM → latent-level VAM: Early methods predict future images or goal images, which are intuitive but redundant; latent-level methods use intermediate denoising features to calculate more efficiently and closer to action-related geometric/relationship clues.

Latent VAM / VPP → VAMPO: VPP already has VPM + AGM pipeline; VAMPO does not change the architecture, but directly optimizes the dynamics accuracy of latent future representation during VPM post-training.

DDPO/GRPO → VAMPO: DDPO writes diffusion denoising as MDP; VAMPO uses this perspective, but designs a Euler Hybrid sampler that is only random in the first step for the robot VPM, and uses expert latent dynamics to define rewards.

4.3 Core design and mathematical derivation

Latent-level VAM basics

This formula describes VAM's two-stage pipeline: first predict the future visual representation, and then convert the predicted representation into actions.

$$(\hat{x}_0, \hat{h})=\mathrm{VPM}_{\theta}(c_v), \qquad \hat{a}_0=\mathrm{AGM}_{\theta}(c_a), \quad c_v=(o, l), \quad c_a=(\hat{h}, l)$$
$o$Current observation, usually from robot camera or multi-view observation.
$l$Language instructions.
$\hat{x}_0$Future latent representation predicted by VPM.
$\hat{h}$Predictive visual feature extracted from VPM denoiser hidden layer for conditional AGM.

VPM's EDM noising and supervised objective

When training VPM, first encode the expert future video into latent, and then add noise according to the continuous noise level to restore the denoiser to clean latent.

$$x_0=\mathcal{E}(N), \qquad x_\sigma=x_0+\sigma\epsilon, \quad \epsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0, \mathbf{I})$$ $$\mathcal{L}_{Video}=\mathbb{E}_{x_0, \epsilon, \sigma_i}\left\|D_{\theta_v}(x_{\sigma_i}, \sigma_i, c_v)-x_0\right\|^2$$
$N$Target future video clip, that is, expert future sequence.
$\mathcal{E}$Pre-trained VAE encoder.
$D_{\theta_v}$VPM denoiser, enter noise latent, noise level and condition $c_v$.

This step corresponds to the likelihood-surrogate training of the original VPM. VAMPO does not delete it, but performs reward-based post-training on it.

Deterministic Euler Discrete solver

Steps from large noise to zero noise during inference; the original solver is deterministic and therefore does not provide the stochastic transition density required by policy gradient.

$$x_{\sigma_{i-1}}=x_{\sigma_i}+(\sigma_{i-1}-\sigma_i)\frac{x_{\sigma_i}-D_{\theta_v}(x_{\sigma_i}, \sigma_i, c_v)}{\sigma_i}$$
$\sigma_I>\cdots>\sigma_0=0$Decrement the noise schedule.
$x_{\sigma_i}$The latent state at the $i$th noise level.

Denoising MDP formulation

Think of the denoising process as an episode: the state is the current noisy latent and conditions, the action is the next latent, and the final reward is only given after the denoising ends.

$$\tau=(\mathbf{s}_I, \mathbf{a}_I, \mathbf{s}_{I-1}, \mathbf{a}_{I-1}, \ldots, \mathbf{s}_0, \mathbf{a}_0)$$ $$\mathbf{s}_i=(x_{\sigma_i}, \sigma_i, c_v), \qquad \mathbf{a}_i=x_{\sigma_{i-1}}, \qquad \mathbf{R}(\mathbf{s}_i, \mathbf{a}_i)=\mathbbm{1}_{[i=0]}r(x_{\sigma_0})$$ $$\mathcal{J}(\boldsymbol{\pi})=\mathbb{E}_{\tau\sim\boldsymbol{\pi}}\left[\sum_{i=0}^{I}\mathbf{R}(\mathbf{s}_i, \mathbf{a}_i)\right]$$
$\boldsymbol{\rho}_0$Initial state distribution, determined by condition distribution, starting timestep $I$ and Gaussian white noise.
$\boldsymbol{P}$State transition after action execution; determines next latent, next noise level, and same condition.
$r(x_{\sigma_0})$terminal reward, evaluates the quality of the final predicted latent.

Euler Ancestral transition and Hybrid sampler

In order to have a computable log probability, VAMPO changes the first step of denoising to a Gaussian SDE transition.

$$x_{\sigma_{i-1}}=x_{\sigma_{i-1}}^{\mathrm{det}}+\sigma_{\mathrm{up}}\epsilon, \quad \epsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0, \mathbf{I})$$ $$x_{\sigma_{i-1}}^{\mathrm{det}}=x_{\sigma_i}+(\sigma_{\mathrm{down}}-\sigma_i)\frac{x_{\sigma_i}-D_{\theta_v}(x_{\sigma_i}, \sigma_i, c_v)}{\sigma_i}$$ $$p_\theta(x_{\sigma_{i-1}}\mid x_{\sigma_i}, c_v)=\mathcal{N}\left(x_{\sigma_{i-1}}; x_{\sigma_{i-1}}^{\mathrm{det}}, \sigma_{\mathrm{up}}^2\mathbf{I}\right)$$
$\sigma_{\mathrm{up}}$stochastic noise scale.
$\sigma_{\mathrm{down}}$The effective noise level used by deterministic update.
$p_\theta$GRPO/PPO ratio required transition density.

The key constraint of the Hybrid sampler: there is randomness only in the first step, and the remaining denoising steps return to deterministic Euler ODE.

$$p(\mathbf{s}_{0: I-1}, \mathbf{a}_{1: I}\mid\mathbf{s}_I)=\boldsymbol{\pi}^{eas}(\mathbf{a}_I\mid\mathbf{s}_I)\prod_{i=1}^{I-1}\delta\left(\mathbf{a}_i-f_{\mathrm{eds}}(\mathbf{s}_i)\right)$$

The reason given in the paper is: if SDE is added to all denoising steps, the terminal reward will be used by subsequent denoising actions unrelated to action modeling, resulting in credit assignment problems and reward hacking; while AGM uses early denoising features, so concentrating randomness in the first step is closer to action-relevant representation.

Reward and GRPO objective

The reward directly compares the predicted future latent and the expert future latent: L1 constrains the absolute error, and cosine constrains the directional consistency.

$$r=-\lambda_{L1}\|x_{\sigma_0}-x_0\|_1+\lambda_{\mathrm{cos}}\frac{x_{\sigma_0}\cdot x_0}{\|x_{\sigma_0}\|\|x_0\|}$$ $$A_g=\frac{r_g-\mathrm{mean}(\{r_1, \ldots, r_G\})}{\mathrm{std}(\{r_1, \ldots, r_G\})}$$
$G$Number of candidates rolled out under the same conditions; appendix is set to 8.
$x_0$Expert future clip is the ground-truth latent obtained by VAE encoder.
$A_g$Within-group normalization advantage; GRPO does not require a separate value model.

GRPO uses a clipped probability ratio so that the new VPM does not deviate too far from the old VPM at one time.

$$J(\theta)=\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{a}_{I, g}\sim\boldsymbol{\pi}_{\theta_{\mathrm{old}}}}\left[\frac{1}{G}\sum_{g=1}^{G}\min\left(\rho_{I, g}A_g, \mathrm{clip}(\rho_{I, g}, 1-\epsilon_c, 1+\epsilon_c)A_g\right)\right]$$ $$\rho_{I, g}=\frac{\boldsymbol{\pi}_\theta(\mathbf{a}_{I, g}\mid\mathbf{s}_{I, g})}{\boldsymbol{\pi}_{\theta_{\mathrm{old}}}(\mathbf{a}_{I, g}\mid\mathbf{s}_{I, g})}$$
Pseudocode of training process 
Input: old VPM policy pi_old, dataset D, group size G
Initialize pi_theta = pi_old
for iteration t = 1..T:
  sample batch of conditions c_v=(o,l) and expert future latents x_0
  sample shared initial noise x_sigma_I
  for g = 1..G:
    rollout hybrid sampler:
      first denoising step: Euler Ancestral SDE
      remaining steps: Euler Discrete ODE
    obtain candidate latent x_sigma_0^g
    compute reward r_g from latent L1 and cosine similarity
  compute group-normalized advantages A_g
  update theta with clipped GRPO objective
  periodically set pi_old = pi_theta

5. Experiment

5.1 Experimental setup

simulation environment.The main evaluation is CALVIN ABC$\to$D: trained on environment ABC, tested on unseen D, tested visual appearance and layout generalization, and long-range language conditioning task performance. The paper also uses L-CALVIN to expand the sequence length from 5 to 10 steps to test longer horizons.

Baselines. The Base policy is VPP because it is a strong VPM-based VLA. Comparative methods include VLM-based VLAs (OpenVLA, OpenVLA-OFT, $\pi_0$, $\pi_{0.5}$) and VPM-based VLAs (UniPi, SuSIE, CLOVER, GR-1, VidMan, Seer, Seer-Large, VPP, Tri-VLA).

Real environment.The real system is an Agibot Genie 01 dual-arm robot with a total of 14 DoF arms and a 2-DoF gripper at each end. Perception is provided by three RGB-D cameras: a head-mounted Intel RealSense D455 that provides a third-person global view, and two wrist-mounted Intel RealSense D405s that provide close encounters and capture geometric details. Each task collects 200 high-quality teleoperated demonstrations.

Figure S. Examples of real robot tasks: messy grasping, single-arm precision placement, and two-arm coordinated grasping and placement.

6. Result analysis and discussion

6.1 Analysis of the results given in the paper

The paper's answer to Q1 is that the L1 distance between latent dynamics and ground-truth latent after VAMPO training decreases overall, qualitatively reducing hallucination, correcting planning and action prediction errors. For Q2, the table shows that VPM improvements can be converted into action execution gains, especially when AGM is also trained on the visual dynamics generated by post-trained VPM, the gains are significantly expanded.

For Q3, the paper uses the effective rank analysis of Jacobian $\mathbf{J}=\partial\mathbf{a}/\partial\mathbf{v}$. Higher Avg. ER/ERR means AGM relies on more independent visual directions when generating actions; VAMPO improves from 29.28 / 0.0603 to 43.88 / 0.0814, which is interpreted by the author as richer vision-action coupling.

For Q4, the author emphasizes that although VAMPO is built on VPP, it does not require additional network components or additional data to surpass VPP and Tri-VLA; the gain is more obvious in the L-CALVIN long-range setting, indicating that long-range manipulation is more sensitive to the quality of visual dynamics.

For Q5, the author explains the component effects through four groups of ablation: GRPO is better than DDPO, 1-step SDE is better than 5-step SDE, latent reward is better than pixel reward, there are RL fluctuations in post-training steps but in the end 1400 steps are the best.

6.2 The future direction clearly stated in the paper

In Conclusion, the author does not list an independent list of future work, but gives a directional judgment: video-based robot learning and VLA systems may need to move from likelihood-only training to direct optimization of control-relevant predictive signals.

7. Analysis, Limitations and Boundaries

7.1 The most valuable part of this paper

Within the paper's own experimental and methodological framework, the core value is to further advance "future visual representation is useful for control" to "future visual representation should be directly optimized according to control-related rewards". VAMPO does not stop at larger models or more data, but turns VPM's denoising trajectories into GRPO-able objects, and uses expert latent dynamics as non-adversarial, verifiable rewards.

7.2 Why the results hold up

The evidence chain is relatively complete: first use L1 latent evaluation and visualization to prove that visual dynamics itself is improved; then use frozen/original AGM to compare with post-trained AGM to show how this improvement is transmitted to action; then use ER/ERR to explain that vision-action coupling becomes stronger; and finally use CALVIN, L-CALVIN, real Agibot tasks and four types of ablation to rule out the explanation of "just an accidental improvement in benchmark performance".

7.3 Limitations explicitly acknowledged or experimentally exposed by the authors

  • RL post-training has fluctuations.The paper clearly states in the ablation of post-training steps that the training process is somewhat unstable, and performance drops will occur in the middle; this comes from the stochasticity of RL.
  • SDE is position sensitive.The authors point out that applying SDE in subsequent denoising steps can lead to reward hacking, because subsequent perturbations may optimize reward without actually improving action-relevant early representation.
  • VPM improvement requires AGM uptake.The improvement is smaller when only post-training VPM but keeping original AGM; larger improvement requires post-training VPM + post-training AGM.
  • Texture details are not perfect.Appendix One-step prediction visualization Textures and fine-grained details are not perfectly precise, but the trajectories and physical interactions are consistent.

7.4 Applicable boundaries

VAMPO's settings are suitable for latent-level VPM-based VLA, especially the future visual representation pipeline that will be used by AGM to generate actions. The reward requires latent $x_0$ encoded by expert future clip, so it is post-training based on demonstration/video data, not online RL without expert future trajectory.

The experiment mainly covers the tabletop tasks of CALVIN ABC$\to$D, L-CALVIN and Agibot Genie 01. Although the paper also shows qualitative visualization of Flexiv dual-arm robot and VidowX, the main numerical evaluation focuses on the CALVIN and Agibot tasks.
